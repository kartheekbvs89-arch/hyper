Assignment Overview

In this assignment you will build a realistic mixed-type pipeline on the Adult Census dataset, tune it with RandomizedSearchCV, and then quantify the optimistic bias of non-nested CV by running nested CV on the same setup. The bonus task adds joblib.Memory caching and measures the resulting speedup.

Designed for 3–4 hours of work. The slow step is the nested CV — it takes roughly 5× the wall-clock of non-nested CV because each outer fold re-runs the full inner search. Plan accordingly: start the bonus task in the background while you write up your analysis of tasks 1–5.

Dataset

We use the Adult Census dataset (fetch_openml(name='adult', version=2)) — ~49,000 rows, 14 mixed-type features (5 numeric, 9 categorical), binary target (income <=50K vs >50K). It is the canonical "mixed-type tabular" benchmark: high-cardinality categoricals (native_country, occupation), missing values (workclass, occupation), and imbalanced classes (~76% vs ~24%). Adult Census is rich enough to make every preprocessing choice matter and large enough that the nested CV takes 5–15 minutes — long enough to be a real experiment, short enough to run on a laptop.

💡
Use n_jobs=1 for the outer CV

Set n_jobs=1 on the outer cross_val_score and let n_jobs=-1 on the inner RandomizedSearchCV do the parallelism. Nesting two levels of joblib.Parallel can oversubscribe your CPU and actually slow things down. The rule: parallelize the innermost level only.
