Assignment Overview

In this assignment you will reproduce the core empirical claim of Smith & Topin (2017): different LR schedules reach meaningfully different accuracies on the same model+dataset, even when trained for the same number of epochs. You will build a small CNN on CIFAR-10 (or Fashion-MNIST for speed), train it under five schedules — fixed LR, step decay, cosine annealing, cosine warm restarts (SGDR), and One Cycle — and plot val accuracy curves to compare convergence speed, final accuracy, and stability across three random seeds. The bonus task asks you to add linear warmup in front of cosine decay and vary the warmup duration.

Plan for 3–4 hours. Roughly: 30 minutes to build and validate the CNN, 1–1.5 hours to train the five schedules (each takes 5–15 minutes on Fashion-MNIST, longer on CIFAR-10), 30 minutes for the comparison plot and the 3-seed stability run, and 30–60 minutes on the bonus. If training time is the bottleneck, use Fashion-MNIST and reduce the CNN size — the qualitative schedule comparison is the point, not absolute accuracy.

Dataset & Model

Use either torchvision.datasets.CIFAR10 (32×32 RGB, 50k train / 10k test, 10 classes) or torchvision.datasets.FashionMNIST (28×28 grayscale, 60k train / 10k test, 10 classes). Fashion-MNIST is faster to train and sufficient for the schedule comparison; CIFAR-10 is more representative of real image work. Build a small CNN — two conv blocks (Conv → BN → ReLU → MaxPool) followed by two FC layers — that reaches 90%+ on Fashion-MNIST in 15-20 epochs.

Task 2 — Train Under Five Schedules

Train the model from scratch five times, once per schedule, each for the same number of epochs (15–20 is reasonable for Fashion-MNIST). The five schedules are: (a) fixed lr=1e-3, (b) step decay StepLR(step_size=5, gamma=0.5), (c) cosine annealing CosineAnnealingLR(T_max=EPOCHS, eta_min=1e-5), (d) SGDR CosineAnnealingWarmRestarts(T_0=5, T_mult=1, eta_min=1e-5), (e) One Cycle OneCycleLR(max_lr=3e-3, total_steps=EPOCHS*len(train_loader), pct_start=0.3). For each run, record the per-epoch val accuracy. Use Adam as the optimizer for all runs.

Hint: OneCycleLR steps per-batch, others per-epoch

The first four schedulers are stepped once per epoch (outside the batch loop, inside the epoch loop). OneCycleLR is stepped once per batch (inside the batch loop, immediately after optimizer.step()). Mixing these up is the most common bug in this assignment. Wrap your training loop in a function that takes the scheduler type as an argument and handles the stepping logic correctly.
